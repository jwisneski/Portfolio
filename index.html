What are we being asked to solve? And is that the core issue?



Sometimes they're the same thing but often they're not. Looking at the problem from a customer's perspective can make the difference between useful sucess and easy failure.

Getting the ideas out there



What does it look like? What are different views or states? Does it actually make sense? Sketches communicate ideas in a way that words alone can't.

What's out there today?



Design patterns can be used and extended (or ignored) based on a customer's mental model. Understanding the patterns can help frame expectations.

Testing... repeatedly



An opportunity to test should never be turned down. Leaving out some of the most obvious components can force the conversation and uncover solutions that could never exist in the current line of thinking.

Just do it.



No design is succcessful if it's left on the cutting room floor. The real magic of what we do is that ideas can suddenly exist and become a reality that solves a problem or makes someone's life just a little better.
