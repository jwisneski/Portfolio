The
Savings Launcher

Discounts without the Kohl's Math

Why we did it

“I shouldn't need a Ph.D. to check out”
‐ Kohl's customer comment

The Kohl's discount model is complex. Customers can stack multiple offers but various restrictions apply. Unlike most other retailers, a customer actually has to work to get the best deal at Kohl's.



What we did


We attempted to bring new customers in, and increase overall conversion, by offering a shortcut to savings. The Savings Launcher offers newer customers the option to bypass the complexity of discounts by flipping a single switch.

This project has increased sales by over $100 million since April of 2017.

What went well


This project was so much fun! Not only did we talk to customers but we set a precedent for how the Customer Experience team used customer feedback.

As an added bonus this has lifted sales by a considerable margin, currently over $100,000,000 in the first year alone. The project was also able to inform other projects across the experience, not just in checkout.

What didn't go well


This was a huge example of "ask for forgiveness", we just started doing it. As we moved along we brought other designers and teams in but that caused some headaches and a lot of redundant conversations.

How I'm trying to improve it


I think setting the expectation and communicating ahead of time could have resolved some issues here. As I took on more a lead role and coordination became huge, I've started to have more convesations about expectations early.
