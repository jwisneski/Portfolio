GroupConnect

Summer 2014 • In-House Interaction Design

Connecting marketers to customers with messaging apps like Line, WhatsApp and WeChat.

This time it's personal

  • Part of a 3 month internship
  • Worked closely with a Senior Designer
  • I made wires and fleshed out interactions, he provided guidance and visual design
  • Targeted younger segments, especially in Asian markets.

Why?

Customers can get useful information and ask questions without ever leaving their messaging app. This lets companies engage customers with a two-way conversation over the traditional mass email model.

Before GroupConnect

  • SMS messages were a used for marketing
  • They were static, clunky and not particularly inspiring
  • Messages were just text with no audio, video, stickers or emojis that are popular today
  • SMS works well for quick, to-the-point interactions

Getting started

The shotgun approach


  • This was my first real design project
  • I was eager to prove myself but I ended up focusing on breadth rather than depth
  • I had to learn to take a step back and ask questions before diving in and designing the whole system

Settling into a rhythm

The assembly line approach


After a design review with the broad ideas I realized that this was a design marathon and not a design sprint. I took the feedback and with the help of my team I was abled to step back and attatck one piece of the project at a time.

Follow messages are like forms

Each field is a separate message

Success means all fields are filled in

The customer can also abandon

Outbound messages are like sending a mass email

Response messages are what makes this really useful

These rely on Keywords, like language

Different Keywords trigger different responses

Segments give customers a truly personal message

Author's commentary

About future iterations



A lot of the features here are starting to make their way into other messaging apps and chat bots. Looking back at the project with that in mind, there’s a few opportunities to update this with more modern patterns.

From the message app with a friend, family member or significant other, a customer can

  • Book a flight and a hotel
  • Plan a trip
  • Schedule a meeting
  • Buy a gift

The big pitch

The Mad Men approach



We tried for two months to nail down a design for a timeline summary. If we didn’t come up with something the feature would be delayed and we’d have to revisit other parts of the experience.

At 11am on the last day I pitched the design that would peak the interest of our partners.

The initial idea was to have as much information as possible. We looked at how the feature would be used and realized that there would be a lot of messages and information overload would happen almost immediately.

We landed somewhere in the middle, using color for shorthand and hiding information that may not be needed at a glance.

To avoid information overload we agreed that not every message would be displayed but instead just 3 -5 at a time. Each message would display for a short time and deflate to make room for new messages. Clicking on a message will bring up more information.

At a glance a marketer can see if the messages are resulting in more errors than normal and quickly fix the problem.
