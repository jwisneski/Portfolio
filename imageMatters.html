Company Website

Summer 2015 • Contract Web Design

An easy to update, responsive Wordpress website and style guide for a small consultancy.

More than a minor update

  • Part of a 3-month contract
  • I worked as the sole member of the design team
  • Designed and built a 28 page website
  • Imagery and content were out of scope

Later the careers section was added to the scope after talking to the HR Lead about the importance of new talent.

Why?

Image Matters is a small contractor and the teams are busy. The website was designed so employees without a coding background could make changes.

Careers matter

The Careers Page was added later but it was one of the biggest updates on the site.

  • Each page was designed with mobile in mind
  • The applications are made with a code-free template taht can be reused for new jobs
  • I worked closely with the head of HR to balance benefits, job descriptions and culture

A modern homepage

  • The whole site moved from a 1000px static container to a 100% wide fluid page
  • A more modern layout and a clear Call to Action helped to organize content
  • We added information about the company, achievements and projects to add credibility without having to click

Cleaning up the interior

  • To save some money I designed backgrounds for the page titles myself
  • Since content was out of my hands I couldnt cut down on text so much as space it out

And a style guide

  • As part of the deliverables I created a style guide with code samples, voice suggestions and terminology
  • This was to help communicate rationale as well as a “how-to” for many of the commonly used sections
  • The guide was passed on to help future designers and current employees
